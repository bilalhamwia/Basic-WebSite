The livelihoods team distributes sheep to affected families in the fort

Over the course of three consecutive days, the Syrian Arab Red Crescent Society (HRC) Livelihood Team in Homs completed the distribution of ewes (400 pregnant sheep and feed) in cooperation with the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies.

The ring was distributed to 200 families of families returning to the Hossn area in Homs, each of which had two loaves and 200 kilos of feed.

The objective of the project is to build the capacity of local rural communities and restore life cycle to affected areas as well as to improve the status of living families and convert them from beneficiaries to producers.

In addition, the project team will follow up on the beneficiaries through regular visits to the beneficiaries to view the health status of the sheep, training the beneficiaries after the birth of ewes on how to take care of the sheep, and the veterinarian will conduct periodic tours to examine the sheep and give the necessary vaccinations.
A convoy of humanitarian aid

The Syrian Arab Red Crescent branch in Damascus (the Disaster Relief Unit), in cooperation with the United Nations offices, delivered a humanitarian aid convoy to some 35,000 people in the areas of Yilda, Babila and Beit Sahm on 8 April 2017.

The convoy consisted of 21 trucks (7000 food baskets, 7000 health baskets, baby nutrition items, medicines and medical items).
Responses of ambulance teams

The first aid teams responded to reports of several shells falling in both the Mohajer and al-Maliki areas, where emergency service was provided for three cases on the ground.
10 thousand bottles of water reach the clinic in the city of Tel

The drinking water and sanitation team of the Syrian Arab Red Crescent branch in the countryside of Damascus delivers clean drinking water to the comprehensive clinics center in the city of Al-Tal periodically, and the number of beneficiaries is 1500 people per month. On 21/2/2017, the team delivered about 10,000 water bottles, each with 1.5 liters of water donated by the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC).
